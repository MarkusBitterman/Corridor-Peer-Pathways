# Refugees, New Americans, and Displaced Persons

## üåç Welcome to Eastern Iowa

Eastern Iowa is home to vibrant refugee and immigrant communities‚Äîincluding Somali, Sudanese, Congolese, Bosnian, and many other communities who have built lives in Johnson and Linn Counties.

This section exists to:
- **Acknowledge** that displacement, resettlement stress, and cultural transition are real recovery contexts
- **Center** language access, trust-building, and community-based support
- **Connect** you to local resources designed with your needs in mind

Peer support here means **meeting people where they are**‚Äîwithout requiring fluency in systems that were never designed for them.

This section emphasizes **agency, safety, and belonging**.

## ü§ù Peer Support Philosophy

> **You are a person.  
> Your experiences are valid.  
> You belong here.**

Peer support for refugees and new Americans means:
- **Meeting you where you are**‚Äînot where systems expect you to be
- **Respecting** that you are the expert on your own life
- **Acknowledging** that building a life in a new country is hard work
- **Walking alongside**‚Äînot directing from above

Recovery does not require fluency in English or in American systems.  
It requires **connection, safety, and someone who sees you**.

## üìù Important Information

### Your Rights
- You have the right to access emergency medical care regardless of immigration status
- You have the right to send your children to public school regardless of immigration status
- You have the right to be free from discrimination based on national origin
- You have the right to remain silent if questioned about immigration status

### Safety Planning
- Keep important documents (passports, I-94, work permits) in a safe, accessible place
- Have emergency contact numbers memorized or written down
- Know where to find legal help before you need it
- Connect with community members who can support you

### Trust Your Community
Eastern Iowa's refugee and immigrant communities are strong. Lean on each other. Build networks of mutual support.
