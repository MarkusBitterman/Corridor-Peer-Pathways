# Utility Assistance Resources

## Low-Income Home Energy Assistance Program (LIHEAP)

### About LIHEAP
- **Administered by:** Iowa Department of Health and Human Services
- **Services:** One-time payment toward home heating for qualified low-income households
- **Eligibility:** Both homeowners and renters
- **Application Period:** November 1 – April 30 (early applications for seniors/disabled start in October)
- **Additional Benefits:** May trigger eligibility for weatherization assistance to improve home energy efficiency
- **Required Documents:** Proof of income, social security numbers, utility bills

### State Online Application
- **Website:** [liheap-apply.hhs.iowa.gov/s/](https://liheap-apply.hhs.iowa.gov/s/)
- **State Info Line:** (515) 281-3861
- **National Directory:** [liheap.org/directory](https://www.liheap.org/directory)

### Iowa Utilities Commission
- **Website:** [iuc.iowa.gov/customer-assistance](https://iuc.iowa.gov/customer-assistance/how-do-i-apply-energy-assistance-liheap)

## Winter Disconnection Protection

### Important Information
- If you are facing disconnection, **apply for LIHEAP and notify your utility provider**
- You are typically **protected from shutoff during winter (November 1 – April 1)** if your LIHEAP application is in process
- Contact your utility company immediately if you receive a disconnection notice

## Tips for Getting Help

- **Apply early:** LIHEAP funds are limited and distributed first-come, first-served
- **Call your county's community action agency (HACAP) for the fastest help**
- **If you need help navigating programs, 211 operators can guide you**
- **Keep all utility bills and income documentation ready when you apply**
- **Most programs are available once per year, so plan accordingly**
