# Know Your Rights: ICE & Immigration Enforcement

## üõ°Ô∏è Your Constitutional Rights

### The Right to Remain Silent
- You have the right to remain silent and do not have to answer questions about where you were born, your immigration status, or how you entered the United States.
- If you choose to remain silent, say: **"I am exercising my right to remain silent."**

### The Right to Refuse Consent to Search
- You have the right to refuse consent to a search of yourself, your belongings, your car, or your home.
- If asked, you can say: **"I do not consent to a search."**

### The Right to an Attorney
- You have the right to speak with an attorney before answering questions.
- You can say: **"I want to speak with a lawyer."**

### The Right to a Hearing Before a Judge
- If you are arrested, you have the right to a hearing before an immigration judge.
- You have the right to present evidence and call witnesses.

## üè† If ICE Comes to Your Home

### Know This First
- **ICE cannot enter your home without your consent OR a valid judicial warrant signed by a judge.**
- An **administrative warrant (Form I-200 or I-205)** is NOT enough to enter without consent.
- A **judicial warrant** will have a judge's signature and be issued by a court.

### What to Do
1. **Do not open the door.** You can speak through the door or a window.
2. **Ask:** "Do you have a warrant signed by a judge?"
3. **If they say yes:** Ask them to slide it under the door or hold it to a window. Look for a judge's signature.
4. **If they do not have a judicial warrant:** You can say, **"I do not consent to your entry."**
5. **Stay calm.** Do not lie, but remember you have the right to remain silent.

### Important
- ICE may claim they have a warrant when they do not. Ask to see it.
- Do not sign anything without speaking to an attorney.
- If agents enter without consent or a valid warrant, stay calm, do not resist, and note what happened for your attorney.

## üöó If Stopped by ICE in Public or While Driving

### Your Rights
- You have the right to remain silent.
- You have the right to refuse consent to search your vehicle.
- If you are a passenger, you can ask: "Am I free to leave?"
- If you are driving, you may be required to show your driver's license and registration, but you do not have to answer questions about your immigration status.

### What to Do
1. **Stay calm.** Keep your hands visible.
2. **You do not have to answer questions** about where you were born or your immigration status.
3. **You can say:** "I am exercising my right to remain silent."
4. **You can say:** "I do not consent to a search."
5. **If arrested:** Ask for an attorney. Do not sign anything.

## üíº If ICE Comes to Your Workplace

### Know This
- ICE may conduct workplace raids, but employers must receive proper documentation.
- You still have constitutional rights at work.

### What to Do
1. **Stay calm.** Do not run or resist.
2. **You have the right to remain silent.** You do not have to answer questions about your immigration status.
3. **You can say:** "I am exercising my right to remain silent."
4. **If detained:** Ask for an attorney. Do not sign anything.
5. **Note:** Employers cannot legally retaliate against you for exercising your rights.

## üìã If You Are Detained

### Immediate Steps
1. **Say:** "I want to speak with a lawyer."
2. **Say:** "I am exercising my right to remain silent."
3. **Do not sign anything** you do not understand.
4. **Do not provide false information.** Remain silent instead.
5. **Memorize or carry** your emergency contact's phone number.

### Know This
- You have the right to a hearing before an immigration judge.
- You have the right to present evidence and call witnesses.
- You have the right to appeal certain decisions.
- You may be eligible for bond (release while your case proceeds).

## üë®‚Äçüë©‚Äçüëß Protecting Your Family

### Make a Family Safety Plan
1. **Memorize important phone numbers** (attorney, family member, trusted friend).
2. **Carry emergency contact information** at all times.
3. **Know where important documents are** (birth certificates, passports, immigration papers).
4. **Designate a trusted person** to care for your children if needed.
5. **Create a power of attorney** for child care and finances.
6. **Talk to your children** in an age-appropriate way about what to do.

### Important Documents to Keep Safe
- Passports
- Birth certificates (especially for U.S. citizen children)
- Immigration documents (I-94, work permits, green cards)
- Medical records
- School records

### If a Parent Is Detained
- Children who are U.S. citizens cannot be deported.
- Family members can contact an immigration attorney.
- Schools cannot deny education to children based on parents' immigration status.

## üè´ Safe Spaces (Sensitive Locations)

### ICE Policy on Sensitive Locations
ICE has a policy of generally **not conducting enforcement actions** at:
- Schools and childcare facilities
- Medical treatment and health care facilities
- Places of worship
- Religious or civil ceremonies
- Public demonstrations

### Important Note
- This is ICE policy, not law‚Äîit can change or be violated.
- If enforcement occurs at a sensitive location, document what happened and contact an attorney.
- These policies are meant to ensure people feel safe accessing essential services.

## üì± What to Do if You Witness ICE Activity

### If You Are Not the Target
1. **Stay calm and observe from a safe distance.**
2. **Document:** Note the date, time, location, and what you see.
3. **If safe:** Record video, but do not interfere with enforcement actions.
4. **Offer support:** Let the person know they have the right to remain silent.
5. **Report:** Contact a local immigrant rights organization.

## üö´ What NOT to Do

- **Do not lie** to immigration officers. Instead, remain silent.
- **Do not provide false documents.** This can result in serious legal consequences.
- **Do not run** from officers. Stay calm.
- **Do not resist** physically. Note what happens and tell your attorney.
- **Do not sign anything** you do not understand, especially a voluntary departure form.
- **Do not open your door** without knowing who is there and what warrant they have.
